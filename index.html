Potential Questions for Intake Form

Project Overview

Project Title: What is the title of your project?

Project Summary: Provide a brief summary of what your project aims to achieve.

Funding Amount: How much funding are you requesting?

Value Framework: Alignment

Alignment with Reports and Inquiries:

How does your project support the recommendations from the Mass Casualty Commission Report and the Desmond Fatality Inquiry Final Report?

How does your project help with the National Action Plan to End Gender-based Violence and the Calls for Justice for Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women and Girls?

How does your project include the principles from the Nova Scotia Home for Colored Children Restorative Inquiry?

Value Framework: Equity

Addressing Inequality:

How does your project consider different social identities (e.g., gender, race, disability, age, sexual orientation)?

Is your project designed to help the most marginalized communities? If so, how?

Does your project consider potential racial disparities? If so, how?

How will your project avoid reinforcing stereotypes and biases or creating further inequities?

Are there any unintended impacts or implications for African Nova Scotian or Indigenous communities? If so, what are they?

Value Framework: Community

Community Engagement:

How have you involved the community in developing your project?

What solutions have been proposed by the community, and how does your project incorporate these solutions?

Is similar work already happening in another community? If so, how does your project differ or complement this work?

How does your project reach and support participation from the community?

Value Framework: Practical Impact

Making a Difference:

How will your project make a difference in the community?

Who are the partners involved in your project, and how do they contribute?

How does your project connect to other services in the community?

Are there other organizations doing similar work? If so, how does your project complement their efforts?

Value Framework: Accountability

Data Collection and Evaluation:

Does your project include plans to collect data? If so, what type of data will be collected?

Will the data collected align with the National Results Framework?

Will the data be broken down to reflect diverse populations?

Does your organization have the capacity to collect and analyze this data? If not, what support do you need?

Is there a plan to share the results with project participants and community partners?

Monitoring and Evaluation:

How do you plan to monitor and evaluate the progress and impact of your project?

What specific activities will you undertake for monitoring and evaluation?

How have you budgeted for monitoring and evaluation activities in your project proposal?

Intended Outcomes and Outputs

Intended Outcomes:

What are the intended outcomes of your project? (e.g., reduction in domestic violence incidents, increased support for survivors)

How will you measure these outcomes?

Intended Outputs:

What are the specific outputs of your project? (e.g., number of workshops held, number of participants reached)

How will you track and report these outputs?
